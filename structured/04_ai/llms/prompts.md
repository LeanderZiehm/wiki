```
I want to investigate [your research question] using [qualitative/quantitative/mixed methods] research. Act as a research methods expert and help me: 1) Determine the most appropriate research design for this question, 2) Identify suitable data collection methods and explain why they’re appropriate, 3) Suggest potential limitations of this approach and how to address them, 4) Recommend sample size and selection criteria, and 5) Outline ethical considerations I need to address.
```


```
I found this data/statistic in my research: [paste the data or describe it]. Help me: 1) Explain what this data actually means in simple terms, 2) Identify what conclusions can and cannot be drawn from this information, 3) Suggest how this data supports or challenges different arguments in my field, 4) Point out any limitations or potential issues with how this data was collected or presented, and 5) Recommend how I can effectively incorporate this data into my academic writing.
```


```
Based on my research topic [your topic] and the main arguments I’ve encountered [briefly describe 2–3 main perspectives], help me identify: 1) What questions remain unanswered in this field, 2) What populations, contexts, or scenarios haven’t been adequately studied, 3) What methodological approaches haven’t been tried, 4) How recent developments might challenge existing conclusions, and 5) What interdisciplinary connections haven’t been fully explored.
```

```
I want to cite this source: [brief description of source and its main point]. In the context of my argument that [your thesis], help me: 1) Determine whether this source should be used for background information, direct support, comparison, or counterargument, 2) Identify the most impactful quote or data point to extract, 3) Suggest how to introduce this citation smoothly in my writing, 4) Recommend whether to cite this source early or later in my argument, and 5) Explain how this citation strengthens my overall credibility.
```

```
I want to explain this complex concept from my research: [describe the concept]. Help me: 1) Break down this idea into its essential components, 2) Suggest analogies or examples that would make it accessible to my intended audience, 3) Identify potential points of confusion and how to address them, 4) Recommend the best order to explain different aspects of this concept, and 5) Suggest transition phrases that would help readers follow my logic.
```


```
I’ve researched [your topic] and found these main perspectives: [list 3–4 different viewpoints or findings]. Help me synthesize this research by: 1) Identifying overarching themes that connect these different perspectives, 2) Explaining how these viewpoints complement or conflict with each other, 3) Suggesting a framework for organizing these ideas coherently, 4) Identifying which perspectives are most strongly supported by evidence, and 5) Proposing how I can contribute something new to this conversation.
```
