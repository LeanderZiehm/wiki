Encryption at Rest

Unsecured APIs and Services
Poorly secured APIs can be exploited to leak data or gain unauthorized access.
Missing authentication, excessive permissions, or lack of rate limiting are common issues.

Malware and Ransomware
Malicious software installed via email attachments, compromised websites, or infected devices.
Can steal data, log keystrokes, or encrypt files demanding ransom.

Man-in-the-Middle (MITM) Attacks
Intercepting communication between two parties to eavesdrop or alter data.
Often occurs on unsecured Wi-Fi networks or via DNS spoofing.
